Dynamic Queue Optimization

Introduction

Optimizing consumer satisfaction and operational efficiency in supermarkets, especially in high traffic situations, depends heavily on effective queue management at cashier counters. In multi cashier systems, where customers frequently choose lines for checkout based only on the number of people waiting and ignore the number of products in each cart, this study tackles a major obstacle. Because of the inefficient queue distribution caused by this behavior, customers wait longer at counters with higher item counts and use counters with lower counts less frequently, which eventually detracts from the overall shopping experience.

In order to improve checkout efficiency, we suggest a dynamic queue management system that makes use of cutting-edge technology like IoT devices and real-time monitoring. Our approach integrates a robust object detection model developed using the PyTorch framework and Hugging Face Transformers library. This method determines the length of lines and the contents of each cart by using CCTV footage to identify and categorize consumers and cashiers.

The system counts the items that are in each customer's shopping basket using Internet of Things (IoT) sensors. It then combines this information with CCTV footage to calculate the best cashier allocation. This system distributes the checkout load more evenly and efficiently by dynamically allocating customers to cashier counters based on the number of customers and the complexity of their transactions.

The processed images, which include visual annotations of queue lengths and the best cashier for each customer, are uploaded to Firebase. As a result, the queue data may be viewed and accessed in real time. This technology offers a faster checkout procedure, which not only increases customer satisfaction overall but also optimizes queue management by cutting down on wait times.

The results of this research show that the application of dynamic queue assignment and real-time item counting has the potential to significantly increase both operational efficiency and customer happiness. This study delivers significant insights into and advances in retail queue management techniques, promoting a more efficient and customer-focused approach in supermarket operations by filling in the gaps in current queue management solutions and offering an inclusive, user-friendly system.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to handle the long queues with multiple cashier systems?


Supermarkets aim to maintain profitability by improving customer happiness and operational efficiency in the current competitive retail scene. Queue management at cashier counters is one of the key operational components that shapes the entire shopping experience. Even with several cashier counters available, consumers frequently experience lengthy wait times and annoyance when checking out, particularly during busy times. The shortcomings of the existing queue management methods and the decision-making habits of customers are the main causes of this inefficiency in queue management. One of the main problems is that patrons usually select cashier counters based only on the length of the queue; they don't consider the quantity of goods in each customer's cart. This oversimplified method results in an under- or underutilized queue distribution, where certain cashier counters with more customers but smaller carts are severely delayed, while others with more customers but smaller carts are not used as much.


Proposed Solution

In this proposed dynamic queue management system aims to address these challenges by optimizing the real-time distribution of customers to cashier counters. By utilizing CCTV footage to determine the length and state of each cashier's line and IoT sensors to track the number of items in clients' shopping carts, the system can consider both the number of consumers and the intricacy of their transactions. This approach is intended to reduce waiting times, optimize resource usage, and improve the general customer experience in supermarkets. The increasing need in the market for inclusive, user-friendly solutions that serve a wide range of demographics highlights the significance of having such a system.

Research Objectives

Develop an IoT-based Real-Time Item Counting System

This focuses on creating a system that uses IoT sensors to count items in customers' shopping carts in real-time. The accurate item count will be transmitted to the central queue management system, enabling it to assess transaction complexity and make informed decisions about customer distribution. This ensures that cashiers handling fewer but larger transactions are appropriately balanced with those handling more frequent, smaller transactions.

Implement CCTV-Based Queue Length Monitoring

This involves deploying CCTV cameras to monitor and analyze the length and state of queues at each cashier counter. By integrating computer vision and image processing techniques, the system will dynamically track the number of customers and their respective queue lengths. This real-time data collection allows the queue management system to respond swiftly to changes, optimizing customer flow and minimizing waiting times.

Integrate Machine Learning Algorithms for Decision Making

This aims to incorporate machine learning algorithms to enhance the decision-making process of the queue management system. These algorithms will analyze historical and real-time data to predict queue dynamics and recommend the best cashier allocation for incoming customers. The adaptive nature of machine learning ensures continuous improvement in system performance, leading to more efficient queue management.

Enhance Real-Time Data Integration for Comprehensive Insights

This focuses on creating a robust system to integrate data from various sources in real-time, such as IoT devices, CCTV footage, and historical transaction data. By ensuring seamless data integration, the system can provide comprehensive insights into queue dynamics and customer behavior. This holistic approach will help in fine-tuning the queue management process and making data-driven decisions to optimize cashier allocation.

Evaluate System Performance in High-Traffic Supermarkets

This involves deploying the queue management system in large-scale supermarkets like Cargills Food City, Keels, SPAR, and LAUGFS in Sri Lanka to assess its effectiveness. Performance metrics such as reduced wait times, improved customer satisfaction, and enhanced cashier utilization will be measured. The evaluation will provide valuable insights into the system's impact and highlight areas for further refinement and scalability.

Methodology

The study employs a systematic approach to develop an optimized queue management system for supermarkets, aimed at reducing lengthy waiting times at cashier counters. The methodology consists of interrelated steps that include problem definition, data collection, system design, implementation, and evaluation. This initial step involves gathering essential data from various sources. IoT devices equipped with sensors on shopping carts provide real-time item counts, while strategically placed CCTV cameras monitor queue lengths. These cameras utilize computer vision techniques to analyze video feeds, counting the number of customers in line. Additionally, historical transaction data, such as transaction times and customer flow patterns, is collected to enhance machine learning models for predicting queue dynamics.

The system integrates data from IoT devices, CCTV cameras, and machine learning algorithms. It includes components for queue length monitoring, data validation, and image processing using libraries like OpenCV and PIL. The queue detection model, based on Microsoft's ResNet-50 architecture, accurately identifies and counts customers. IoT devices transmit items count to the central system, enabling real-time decisions on cashier allocation based on current shopping behavior

Trained machine learning models utilize historical and real-time data to optimize cashier assignments, focusing on minimizing wait times. Input images are processed to detect cashiers and customers, enabling the assignment of the optimal cashier based on queue lengths and item counts. Overall, this methodology aims to enhance supermarket operations through effective queue management, leading to improved customer satisfaction and efficiency.
