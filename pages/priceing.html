Pricing and promotion optimization

Introduction

This paper focuses on advanced machine learning techniques in price and promotional strategy optimization at Spar Supermarket. The focus of the study is mainly on the integration of neural collaborative filtering and sentiment analysis to form a hybrid rating system that would increase the accuracy of recommended products and enhance the effectiveness of promotional targeting. Through this research, patterns of consumer behavior and preferences have been unraveled from user interaction data and customer reviews, hence more personalized recommendations.

This paper proposed the use of deep learning through neural collaborative filtering, since it captures very complex relationships between users and products on which, eventually, high prediction accuracy will emerge as compared to traditional recommender systems. This has further been enhanced by a bidirectional LSTM-based sentiment analysis that refines the rating of the products by statuses, thus capturing the qualitative feedback from customer reviews themselves. What combines to make for a dynamic pricing model is the adjustment of discounts based on overall product ratings for the optimization of inventory management and sales performance.

Empirical results indicate that the proposed hybrid rating system significantly enhances customer satisfaction and boosts sales. Filtering out items previously purchased, this recommends mechanism will ensure new product suggestions to customers, thus keeping the relevance of recommendations and encouraging the discovery of new items.

It thus provides valuable lessons on customer-centric marketing and data-driven decision making, which can transform retailer strategies. The findings provide a generalizable and flexible framework for retailers seeking to enhance operational efficiency and maximize revenue through optimized pricing and promotion strategies.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

The proposed problem in this research focuses on optimizing pricing and promotion strategies for a large-scale supermarket. With a diverse customer base and an extensive range of products, the supermarket faces challenges in offering personalized promotions and dynamic pricing that reflect individual customer preferences and behavior. Traditional pricing strategies often overlook these nuances, leading to missed opportunities to boost sales and enhance customer satisfaction. Additionally, current systems are inadequate in leveraging customer sentiment and purchasing history, leaving a gap in delivering tailored promotions that truly resonate with customers. As a result, the supermarket struggles to optimize its pricing and promotion tactics, which can lead to lost revenue, unsold inventory, and weak customer engagement. To remain competitive, the supermarket requires a system that not only recommends products but also adjusts prices and discounts in real time, based on individual customer preferences and sentiments.


Proposed Solution

The proposed solution involves developing a machine learning-based system that optimizes pricing and promotion strategies by analyzing customer behavior and sentiment. The system is built on two key components: a neural collaborative filtering (NCF) model and a sentiment analysis model. The NCF model uses collaborative filtering techniques to predict customer preferences based on historical data of purchases and ratings. Simultaneously, the sentiment analysis model processes customer reviews to gauge their attitudes toward specific products. These models are then integrated to generate an overall rating for each product, combining both the predicted customer preference (NCF rating) and sentiment score. The overall rating is used to recommend products and determine discount percentages, creating a personalized promotion strategy. Furthermore, the system filters out products the customer has already purchased within a certain time range, ensuring that the recommendations are fresh and relevant. This approach enhances both psychological and business outcomes by improving customer satisfaction through personalized offers while increasing the supermarket’s profitability by promoting products that align with customer interests and behavior. This research is conducted with real-world data from Spar Supermarket, ensuring the model’s applicability in a practical retail environment.

Research Objectives

Develop a Neural Collaborative Filtering (NCF) model to accurately predict customer preferences and item ratings

In developing the NCF model, the purchase history data of Spar Supermarket will be utilized. The most popular technique independent of any content information, which relies on user-item interactions to make recommendations, is Neural Collaborative Filtering. It uses neural networks to capture intricate patterns of user-item interaction and, as a result, provides personalized recommendations based on preferences and behavior

Implement a sentiment analysis model to evaluate customer reviews and feedback, integrating this data into the recommendation system

Sentiment analysis by Natural Language Processing techniques shall be used to get an overview of the customer reviews and feedback. This would involve the development of a sentiment analysis model capable of classifying reviews into positive, negative, or neutral and scoring products on sentiments.

Develop a hybrid model that combines collaborative filtering ratings and sentiment analysis to provide a comprehensive rating for each product

In the hybrid model, ratings returned from the collaborative filtering model will be combined with sentiment scores returned by the sentiment analysis model. This approach will make use of weighted averaging or some other fusion techniques to merge these inputs into a single rating for every product.

Develop a dynamic pricing strategy that adjusts discount percentages based on the overall rating of products from the hybrid model

A dynamic pricing model will thereafter be developed, adjusting discount rates according to the hybrid ratings of products. It should, therefore, implement optimization techniques that find optimal discount levels to maximize sales and profitability while conforming to customer preferences.

Develop a comprehensive recommendation system that integrates NCF, sentiment analysis, and hybrid ratings, and evaluate its impact on customer satisfaction and sales performance

A recommendation system will be developed as an independent software application or module to be integrated into Spar Supermarket. It will be able to give personalized product recommendations using the integrated models and give dynamic pricing suggestions as well.

Methodology

The methodology for "Pricing and Promotion Strategy Optimization" involves developing a recommendation system using collaborative filtering and sentiment analysis. Data from Spar Supermarket, including sales, product ratings, and customer reviews, is collected. Two machine learning models are created: a neural collaborative filtering (NCF) model to predict user-product ratings and a sentiment analysis model to analyze product reviews.

The NCF model, built with TensorFlow, learns the preferences of customers by analyzing past purchase data and generating predicted ratings. The sentiment analysis model, using Bidirectional LSTM, processes customer reviews to determine sentiment scores. The final product rating combines predictions from both models.

The system filters out products the customer has already purchased and recommends new items, while discounts are applied based on the final rating, optimizing both pricing and promotion strategies. This approach enhances customer satisfaction and business outcomes by delivering personalized, data-driven recommendations.
